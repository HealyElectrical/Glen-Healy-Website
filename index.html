About Me

I’m an Electrical Engineering student at BCIT who loves building things that move, sense, and actually work. I take projects end-to-end: defining requirements, designing circuits and PCBs in KiCad, writing embedded firmware, integrating sensors, and modeling parts in CAD (FreeCAD/SolidWorks/Inventor) - then validating everything works together as a complete system. Right now I’m building SmartPost, a smart mailbox capstone I’m pushing toward a real product. The main processor is a Raspberry Pi 4, backed by a Raspberry Pi Pico 2 W that can handle must-work actions for reliability alongside the Pi 4. I’m happiest mixing robotics, automation, and 3D design, but I also genuinely enjoy core electrical design - especially power electronics and power systems (transmission and distribution). On top of that, I have a big interest in agricultural technology, and I’m excited about projects that combine sensing, automation, and energy systems to make real-world operations more efficient. Longer-term, I’m also drawn to renewable energy and electric transportation, from micro e-transit to full EV platforms.
